The Pasig River

The Pasig River spans about 27 kilometers, connecting Laguna de Bay to Manila Bay. It traverses several major cities, including Manila, Makati, and Pasig, making it a central waterway for transportation and commerce. Historically, the river served as a crucial trade route and continues to support local industries and informal settlers reliant on fishing and water resources.

Biodiversity Loss

Decline or extinction of species, habitats, and ecosystems, often caused by human activities such as pollution, deforestation, and climate change. This loss disrupts ecosystems and the services they provide, like clean air, water, and food. The diminishing diversity of life can have far-reaching effects on the balance of nature, reducing resilience to environmental changes.

Historical Significance

The Pasig River was the lifeline of Manila, serving as a major transportation and trade hub. Many historical landmarks, such as Intramuros and the Walled City, are situated along its banks.


Rapid industrialization and population growth after World War II led to the river's decline, with untreated sewage, industrial waste, and urban runoff severely affecting water quality and biodiversity.

About Us

We are a group of students dedicated to protecting the Pasig River, a vital waterway with great environmental, cultural, and historical significance for our communities. Through this website, we aim to inspire meaningful actions that will restore the river to its full potential.

The Problem

The formerly healthy ecology became a highly polluted waterway due to industrialization, population increase, and inadequate waste management. Pasig River was declared biologically dead, meaning it could no longer support aquatic life.

Progress Made

The Pasig River Rehabilitation has seen efforts from various administrations and organizations. These initiatives focused on cleaning the polluted river, addressing urbanization challenges, and raising public awareness. Current efforts, such as the Pasig River Urban Development Project, aim to balance ecological restoration with urban development for improved living conditions and sustainability.
